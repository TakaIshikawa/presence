Rank these candidates from best to worst. For each candidate, assess:

1. ENGAGEMENT_POTENTIAL (most important — counts double): Does this provoke a reaction that demands response?
   - Score 9-10: You feel something — recognition, surprise, disagreement, excitement. You MUST reply or share this with your team.
   - Score 7-8: Strong "yes, exactly" or "wait, no" reaction. You'd like, reply, or bookmark it.
   - Score 5-6: Reads fine but provokes nothing — you'd keep scrolling. No emotional reaction.
   - Score 1-4: Generic wisdom that blends into the feed
   - HOOK: Does the opening line create tension, curiosity, or surprise? A strong hook is essential — if the first line doesn't stop scrolling, nothing else matters.
   - SHAREABILITY: Would someone quote-tweet this or send it to a friend? This matters more than whether the reader can "apply" the insight.
   - PENALIZE: Posts that sound like AI-generated thought-leadership content

2. GROUNDEDNESS: Are all factual claims traceable to the source material?
   - Score HIGH: Uses real details from the commits/prompts
   - Score LOW: Invents metrics, fabricates outcomes
   - AUTOMATIC REJECT: Any post with fabricated statistics not present in source material

3. NARRATIVE_SPECIFICITY: Does the post show a journey the reader can connect with?
   - Score HIGH: Shows the process — what went wrong, what was tried, what surprised you. The reader can see themselves in the story.
   - Score LOW: States a takeaway without showing how you got there
   - NOTE: Concrete details alone don't drive engagement. What matters is whether the details serve a narrative that provokes recognition or surprise.
   - PENALIZE: "AI isn't about X—it's about Y" or any abstract aphorism

4. RAWNESS: Does it feel like a real person, not a content machine?
   - Score HIGH: Messy, honest, specific — "spent 3 hours on the wrong thing"
   - Score LOW: Polished, structured, could be any AI consultant's tweet
   - PENALIZE: Em-dash contrast pivots, "the real X is Y" structures, thought-leader cadence

5. VOICE: Does it match the founder's direct, practical style?
