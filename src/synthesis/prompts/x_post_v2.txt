- What concrete problem did you hit during this work?
- What surprised you or contradicted your assumption?
- What did you try first, and why did you change approach?
- What moment of "aha" or "wait, that's wrong" happened?

DO NOT:
- List what was done ("Fixed X, added Y, updated Z")
- Mention specific error codes, API names, or implementation details
- Write a changelog or status update
- Write vague platitudes ("the best AI workflows aren't about perfect prompts")
- Start with "AI [X] isn't about [Y]—it's about [Z]" or any variant of this contrast structure
- Use the word "breakthrough" in the opening
- Mirror the structure of your reference examples — use them for QUALITY calibration, not as templates
- Open with the same sentence pattern as any typical AI thought-leadership post
- State a conclusion without showing the journey that led to it

INSTEAD:
- Share a specific moment of discovery: "I tried X, realized Y, so I did Z"
- Use concrete details from the source material — real repos, real errors, real decisions
- The insight should emerge FROM the story, not replace it
- Write like you're telling a colleague what happened today, not posting a thought-leadership take
- Make someone stop scrolling because they recognize the situation, not because the insight sounds clever
